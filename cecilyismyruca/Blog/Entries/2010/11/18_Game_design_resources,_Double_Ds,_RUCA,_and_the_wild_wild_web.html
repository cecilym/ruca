Love the Game

 

Game design resources, Double Ds, RUCA, and the wild wild web

 

“Do you know what Delta Gamma Delta really stands for? Damn good dancers, that’s what.” -me, playing myself, but blonde, in To Be or Double D (2008)

http://www.youtube.com/cecilyrk#p/a/u/0/ZumSB5Rlne0



Why did I drop that little gem? Working on RUCA, or should I say working on working on RUCA, has got me feeling nostalgic about crazy creative endeavors gone by. This project is so big in comparison it is daunting to think about. It is comforting to remember that at the time I began working on To Be or Double D, it felt every bit as challenging. I made it then and I will again. Just a few of the many elements dancing around in my mind right now: game company internships, improving my resume, finishing the RUCA game design document, researching game companies who may be interested in it, seeking alternative grant based means of funding, my application for the Master’s of Communication in Digital Media graduate school program at the University of Washington, my music, my software, the artists I’m promoting, the stop motion video I am making for Laptop Battle, Lorn, Mochipet, and the Great Mundane at Chop Suey tomorrow night, saving the million urls I have open right now because my laptop is starting to ghost a little bit and I’m afraid it might crash and my bookmarks folder is a confusing nightmare. I’m gonna go with last things first so that I may share with you some of the cool resources I have found for anyone designing a game.
