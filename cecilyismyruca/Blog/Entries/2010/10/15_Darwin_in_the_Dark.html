Darwin in the Dark

 

Waiting on a quite suburban street corner waiting for Blayne to pick me up earlier tonight, a man and a dog named Darwin walked by. Darwin stopped dead in his tracks, sat down on his hind legs and eagerly stared at me from the sidewalk as his owner tried to keep moving along as they had been walking away.


“My dog would really like to meet you.”


“Um, oh. OK...”


I pet Darwin and told him not to worry, that my friend was just down the road coming. I pointed to the lights of Blayne’s car in the distance and told him that I was OK. This seemed to satisfy him because he got up willingly and joined the side of his owner who said that I had made Darwin’s day.


Thanks for looking out for me, Darwin. You made my day.


* * * *


Softly now doth I forsaketh

Alone in the dark

No regrets come to light

The bedside table where I place it

The sword, the shield rest for the night


This game requires so many pieces

The board must be set up just so

No one doubts the players’ reason

Most simply do not dare to go


Heavily now do my eyes linger

On the nearly finished page

Tears of relief and something colder

Symptoms of the day and age


Standing on the roof

Complaining to the moon

The only time I tell the truth is when

I’m naked in my bedroom


Now I know what he meant

Free or Dead

This my self atonement

Sweet surrender

When I can just be me instead
