Sort of finished work early today. It is approximately 4:30 PM. Although morning pages are supposed to be completed in the morning, I think it is better to do them in the afternoon than not do them at all. Today I received another email looking for the other Charles Dodge. These have been coming in a little more often recently. The worst part is when I get an email from a particularly friendly person and they ask, “And which Charles Dodge are you?” My desired reply is hidden under a thick black box. I’m going to another EURECA conference this month. Just bought my plane ticket. My therapist thinks it might be a good place to meet women, or at least new friends. I got another email from Trevor Paglen today, too. He’s an alright guy. I just don’t think I can help him.


I can’t tell if the journal entries are working yet. This is only my second one. The idea is that instead of fighting my depression, I am going to have fifteen minutes of depression every day. Cloe said I must have the 15 minutes of depression every day, ideally in the morning, or else the $5,000 I paid her will go straight down the drain. During my 15 minutes of depression I am to sit and wallow in my depression. I am to write several pages in a journal during this allotted time. I do not have to specifically write about my depression, but it is important that I wallow in my own depression while I am writing. After the 15 minutes is over, I am to avoid depressed thoughts and behavior patterns for the rest of the day. If I feel the need to be depressed about something, I am allowed to make myself a note in my journal on the next days page about things I would like to focus on being depressed about the next day. In three weeks, Cloe is flying in for the weekend so that we can work in person together. We will meet Saturday, Sunday, and have breakfast Monday morning. Cloe says that she charges so much because one weekend will be enough.


There is one more request that Cloe made of me. I am supposed to create a garden and begin tending to it. I told her that wasn’t really possible in Antarctica and to start an indoor garden would take a lot of equipment and planning. She said, “Even better. Start making  that happen. Everyday, do something to improve your garden, even if that something is reading a page of a gardening book or buying a grow light. Baby steps are perfectly acceptable.” Yesterday I found a website about indoor gardening. They have instructions for starting a closet herb garden. Today I will order lights. I still don’t know what herbs I want to grow. Ordering lights is enough progress for me today. After this fifteen minutes is up, instead of spending time worrying about what direction things are going in like I always do, I will be content with taking one step and resuming my worrying during my 15 minute allocated depression time tomorrow. Time is almost up.
